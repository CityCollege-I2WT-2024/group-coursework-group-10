Milestones in Hip-Hop

1973

DJ Kool Herc throws the first block party in the Bronx, marking the birth of hip-hop.

1979

First hip-hop single, "Rapper's Delight," hits the Billboard Top 40, introducing rap to a global audience.

1982

"The Message" by Grandmaster Flash becomes the first socially conscious rap song, addressing urban issues.

1986

"Straight Outta Compton" by N.W.A. popularizes gangsta rap and West Coast culture.

1996

The East vs. West Coast rivalry culminates with the tragic deaths of Tupac Shakur and Notorious B.I.G.

2000

Eminem's "The Marshall Mathers LP" becomes a commercial and critical success, pushing hip-hop further into mainstream culture.

2012

Kendrick Lamar's "good kid, m.A.A.d city" redefines storytelling and artistry in hip-hop.
