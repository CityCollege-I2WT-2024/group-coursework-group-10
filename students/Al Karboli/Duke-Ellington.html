Duke Ellington's Biography

Born April 29th, 1899 in Washington DC, United States, and died on May 24th, 1974 in New York, United States. Duke Ellington the extremley popular American pianist, and bandleader rose to fame during the early 1900s by leading his group at the Cotton Club in New York. His talent as so unique that he was able to blend and play different styles of music whether that be the classical or the modern Jazz in a harmonized manner. He was so beloved and successful that his work was accredited with 13 Grammys, and even a greater honor which was the Presidential Medal Of Freedom till this day his musical genius is played and beloved by people all over the world which just goes to show how big of an artist he was and as time goes on he will be even greater.
