Miles Davis's Biography

Miles Davis, born May 26th, 1926 in Illonois United States, and died on September 28th 1991 in Calafornia United States, was a musical icon who was talented in many different musical fields, famous for his Trumpet skills, composing, and bandleading. He began practing and playing the trumpet at a very young age which led him to study at the julliard school in New York City. After completeing his studys Miles Davis has had one of the most successful careers in music since it spanned for over 50 years. He was constatnly changig the way artists perfomed Jazz, and he was a pioneer during the majority of his career having started many Jazz movments some are, cool jazz, and bepop. His career continues to inspire musicians all over the world. Some of his albums like "Kind of Blue", and "Bitches Brew" remain til this day important hallmark/milestones of many artists hoping to achieve what he did. These albums among many others help cement Miles Davis as an icon in this field.
